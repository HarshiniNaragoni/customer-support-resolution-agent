# Escalation Procedures

## When to Escalate

### Automatic Escalation Triggers
- Customer explicitly requests a manager or supervisor.
- Legal threats or regulatory complaints.
- Safety concerns or product recalls.
- Repeated unresolved issues (3+ attempts).
- VIP or enterprise customer complaints.

### Agent-Initiated Escalation
- Issue exceeds agent authority or knowledge.
- Policy exception required.
- Technical issue beyond scope.
- Sensitive customer situation.

## Escalation Levels

### Level 1: Senior Agent
- Complex technical issues.
- Policy interpretation questions.
- First-time escalation requests.

### Level 2: Supervisor
- Repeated unresolved issues.
- Customer retention concerns.
- Policy exceptions up to $50.

### Level 3: Manager
- Legal or regulatory issues.
- Large refund amounts (over $50).
- Public relations concerns.

### Level 4: Director
- Executive complaints.
- System-wide issues.
- Strategic customer decisions.

## Escalation Process

### Step 1: Document the Issue
- Summarize the customer's problem.
- List all steps already taken.
- Include customer sentiment.

### Step 2: Attempt Resolution
- Try standard solutions first.
- Offer goodwill gestures if appropriate.
- Set clear expectations.

### Step 3: Transfer
- Warm transfer preferred.
- Brief the receiving agent.
- Stay available if needed.

### Step 4: Follow Up
- Check resolution within 24 hours.
- Ensure customer satisfaction.
- Document final outcome.

## Escalation Contacts

### Support Supervisor
- Available during business hours.
- Handles Level 2 escalations.
- Can authorize refunds up to $50.

### Support Manager
- Available for urgent matters.
- Handles Level 3 escalations.
- Can authorize refunds up to $200.

### Director of Operations
- For critical issues only.
- Handles Level 4 escalations.
- Full authority on all matters.
