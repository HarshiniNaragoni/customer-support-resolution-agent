# Priority Levels

## Priority Definitions

### Low Priority
- **Response Time**: 48 business hours
- **Examples**:
  - General information requests
  - Feature suggestions
  - Non-urgent billing questions
- **Handling**: Standard queue processing

### Medium Priority
- **Response Time**: 24 business hours
- **Examples**:
  - Order status inquiries
  - Simple refund requests
  - Account access issues
- **Handling**: First-come, first-served

### High Priority
- **Response Time**: 4 business hours
- **Examples**:
  - Shipping delays
  - Payment processing errors
  - Product functionality issues
- **Handling**: Expedited queue

### Urgent Priority
- **Response Time**: 1 business hour
- **Examples**:
  - Security breaches
  - System outages
  - Safety concerns
  - Legal threats
- **Handling**: Immediate attention

## Priority Assignment Rules

### Automatic Assignment
- Order issues: Medium
- Refund requests: Medium
- Technical support: Medium
- Complaints: High
- Security issues: Urgent

### Customer-Triggered Escalation
- Customer requests manager: High
- Customer threatens legal action: Urgent
- Customer threatens public posting: High
- Repeat contact (3+): Upgrade by one level

### Time-Based Escalation
- No response in SLA: Upgrade by one level
- Issue unresolved for 48+ hours: Upgrade to High
- Issue unresolved for 72+ hours: Upgrade to Urgent

## Priority Management

### Review Schedule
- Daily review of all open tickets
- Weekly priority adjustment meeting
- Monthly SLA compliance review

### Override Authority
- Agents: Can upgrade by one level
- Supervisors: Can set any priority
- Managers: Can override all priorities
